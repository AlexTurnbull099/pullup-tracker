# Summer Pull-Up Bar Plan — Build Muscle (Late June → September)

**Starting point:** 1–4 strict pull-ups · **Goal:** muscle & size · **Frequency:** most days

---

## The 3 rules this plan runs on

1. **Progressive overload.** Each week you do a little more than the last — more reps, more sets, slower tempo, or eventually added weight. That "little more" is what forces growth.
2. **Volume drives size.** With only a few strict reps in the tank, you can't yet live in the ideal 8–12 rep "size" zone on pull-ups. So you accumulate volume through *many submaximal sets, chin-ups, and negatives* until your strict numbers climb.
3. **Recovery is part of training.** Muscle is built when you rest. That's why "most days" works only if hard days alternate with light days (below). Aim for 7–9 hrs sleep and enough protein — to grow, you need to be *eating enough*, not under-fueling.

---

## The weekly rhythm

Rotate three day-types. Repeat the cycle, take **1 full rest day** whenever you feel beat up (at minimum 1 per week):

**HEAVY → VOLUME → LIGHT → HEAVY → VOLUME → LIGHT → rest**

This hits your back and arms hard twice a week, with lighter "greasing the groove" work in between so you recover while still touching the bar daily.

### 💪 HEAVY day (build the rep)
| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Scapular pulls | 2 × 5 | Hang, pull shoulder blades down, arms straight. Warm-up. |
| **Pull-up negatives** | 4–5 × 1 | Jump/step to the top, lower for a slow 3–5 sec. Your #1 size builder right now. |
| Max pull-ups | 3–4 sets | As many strict as possible, stop 1 shy of failure. Rest 2–3 min. |
| Chin-ups (underhand) | 2–3 sets | Usually you'll get more of these. |
| Push-ups | 3 sets | Balances the pulling. Last set explosive for pop-up power. |
| **Core circuit** | 1 round | 30 reps each: crunches, toe touches, around-the-worlds, half v-sits, scissors, sit-up Russian twists, boat Russian twists — then a 30–45s plank. |

### 🔁 VOLUME day (accumulate reps)
| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Scapular pulls | 2 × 8 | |
| Chin-ups | 4–5 sets | **Submaximal** — if your max is 4, do sets of 2. Rest only 60–90 sec. Tempo: 2 sec up, 2 sec down. |
| Pull-ups | 3–4 sets | Submaximal, same idea. |
| Flexed-arm hang | 3 sets | Hold at the top as long as you can. |
| Push-ups | 3–4 sets | |
| **Core circuit** | 1 round | Same circuit as Heavy day (see above). |

### 🌤 LIGHT day (skill / recover)
| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Easy pull-ups | 5–8 × 1–2 | Half your max, spread across the day if you like. **Never strain.** |
| Dead hangs | 3 × 20–40 sec | Grip + decompresses the spine. |
| Scapular pulls | 3 × 8 | |
| Push-ups | 2–3 sets | Easy. |

---

## The 10-week arc

**Phase 1 — Foundation (Weeks 1–3)**
Nail your form, get comfortable hanging and pulling daily. Lean on negatives and chin-ups. Don't chase numbers yet — build the habit and the base.

**Phase 2 — Build (Weeks 4–7)**
Add a set or a rep to your hard days each week. Push max-effort sets closer to failure (still leave ~1 rep). Goal: roughly **double** your starting strict pull-up number by end of Phase 2.

**Phase 3 — Intensify (Weeks 8–10)**
Make reps harder instead of just more numerous: wider grip, slower 3-sec descents, pause at the top. When a set feels easy, add light weight — a loaded backpack is perfect. Test a new max in the final week.

### How to actually progress each week
> When you can hit the **top of the rep range on every set**, level up: first add reps → then slow the tempo → then add weight (backpack). One small step at a time.

---

## Form cues that matter
- **Full range:** dead hang at the bottom, chin clearly over the bar at the top.
- **Shoulders down and back** — initiate by pulling shoulder blades down, not by yanking with the arms.
- **No kipping/swinging** for size; strict and controlled builds more muscle.
- **Control the descent** — the lowering phase grows as much muscle as the pull. Never just drop.

## 🏄 Surfing focus
This plan is already a strong surf-conditioning program — paddling is fundamentally **lat and back pulling endurance**, which is exactly what all the pull-up and chin-up work builds. A few cues to bias it toward the water:

- **Paddling = pulling endurance.** Lean into your VOLUME days. Submaximal chin-ups and pull-ups with short rest train the muscles to keep firing stroke after stroke — that's what keeps you in the lineup instead of gassing out.
- **Pop-up = explosive push.** On HEAVY days, make your **last push-up set explosive** — push hard enough that your hands leave the floor. That snappy press is the same pattern as popping to your feet on a wave.
- **Shoulder durability.** Don't skip the **scapular pulls** — they're prehab for the exact joints a paddler hammers. Strong, controlled shoulders mean fewer tweaks over a long summer of sessions.
- **Grip endurance.** The **dead hangs** on LIGHT days carry straight over to duck-diving and holding your rails in heavy water.

You don't need to change the cycle — just know *why* each piece matters for surfing and put a little extra intent into those four.

## A note on results
Size comes from the training **plus** enough sleep and enough food, especially protein. If the scale and your reps both aren't moving after a few weeks, eating a bit more is usually the missing piece — building muscle requires fuel, not restriction.

---

*Stick the cycle on repeat, add a little each week, rest when sore, and by September you'll have meaningfully more strict reps and a fuller back, arms, and shoulders to show for it.*
